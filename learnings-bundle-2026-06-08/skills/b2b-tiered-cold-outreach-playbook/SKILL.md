## Documentation
# B2B Tiered Cold Outreach Playbook

A repeatable 3-week sequence for cold B2B email campaigns. Built around tiering the recipient list (A/B/C) so high-value contacts get personal touches and the long tail uses mail merge — without burning the calendar.

## Reply-rate expectations (anchor before you start)

| Tier | Definition | Touch type | Expected replies | Expected real conversations |
|------|------------|------------|------------------|------------------------------|
| A | Top 15-25% — named decision-maker, strategic fit, warm angle | Personal email + LinkedIn DM | 25-50% reply | 2-3 calls per 15 |
| B | Middle 50% — role-based or info@ address, fits ICP | Mail merge with personalized tokens | 3-8% reply | 1 quality conversation per 30 |
| C | Bottom 25% — weak fit, peripheral target | Mail merge only, low effort | 0-7% reply | rare |

For a 60-supplier campaign expect 5-10 replies total and 3-4 real conversations. That's a successful outcome.

## Tier-A personal email template

```
Subject: Quick question about {Company}'s {Product} line

Hi {First_Name},

I'm building a fence-type calculator network that connects contractors with local material suppliers — and your {Specific_Product/Range} kept coming up in research as a category leader.

The pitch in one sentence: contractor enters dimensions, gets a quote using YOUR product codes (no SKU re-mapping), orders directly from {Company}. Lead-gen for you, fast accurate take-offs for the contractor.

Two things I'd love your take on:
1. Is {Department/Role} the right person to talk to, or should I be reaching out to someone else?
2. Would a 15-min call next week make sense to see if this fits your distribution model?

Either way, happy to send a wireframe of what a {Company}-branded calculator would look like.

{Sign-off}
{LinkedIn URL}
```

## Tier-B mail-merge template

```
Subject: Calculator network for {Category} contractors

Hi {First_Name},

Quick note — I'm building Anyfence, a network of free fence-type calculators that contractors use to spec materials. The calculator returns a quote using the SUPPLIER's product codes (yours stay yours, no re-mapping).

Every other AU calculator I've seen is locked to a single supplier; every contractor quoting tool needs manual material counting. Anyfence closes both gaps.

If this is interesting, I can send a short overview (one page) and a wireframe showing how {Company}'s product codes would render in a contractor's view.

Worth a 10-min look?

{Sign-off}
```

## Day-7 follow-up (highest unlock — drives forwarded intros)

```
Subject: Re: {Original_Subject}

Hi {First_Name},

Following up on the note from last week. No worries if it's not a priority — but if {Department} isn't the right team, could you point me at who'd own a distribution/integrations question like this?

Either direction helps me a lot.

{Sign-off}
```

## Day-14 break-up email (counterintuitively highest reply rate)

```
Subject: Closing the loop

Hi {First_Name},

Two notes, no reply yet — I'll close my file on this. If timing improves, I'm at {email}.

One thing in case it's useful: we found that {Specific_Insight_Relevant_To_Their_Business} — happy to share the underlying research if you ever want a peek.

All the best,
{Sign-off}
```

The "closing the loop" line is the magic — it removes the obligation to reply and that's exactly why people reply.

## Mail-merge personalization tokens

Minimum tokens for B-tier merge to feel hand-written:
- `{First_Name}` — recipient first name
- `{Company_Name}` — exact business name (no "Inc/Ltd/Pty Ltd")
- `{Category}` — the category they're known for (e.g. "Colorbond", "Pool fencing")
- `{Specific_Product}` — one product the email references by name
- `{Geographic_Note}` — "your NSW depot" or "your VIC manufacturing facility"

If your CSV is missing more than 2 of these for any row, drop them to C-tier or skip — generic mail merge is worse than nothing because it teaches the recipient to ignore future emails from you.

## Subject-line conventions

- Tier A: question format → "Quick question about {Company}'s {Product} line"
- Tier B: value format → "Calculator network for {Category} contractors"
- Tier C: short generic → "{Category} suppliers and contractors"
- Follow-up: `Re:` prefix on the original
- Break-up: "Closing the loop" — universally high-performer

Never use: "Just checking in" (signals laziness), ALL CAPS, emojis, "important" markers.

## NDA template (mutual, AU jurisdiction, plain English)

Use a one-page mutual NDA for any conversation where you'd discuss product roadmap or pricing logic. Key clauses:

1. Both parties acknowledge mutual exchange of Confidential Information
2. Confidential Information defined as marked confidential OR reasonably understood as confidential
3. Standard exclusions (publicly known, independently developed, required by law)
4. 2-year confidentiality term post-disclosure
5. Governing law: New South Wales, Australia (or whichever state suits you)
6. No lawyer review required for the standard form — just countersigned scan back

## Three-week sequence (calendar view)

| Day | Tier A action | Tier B action | Tier C action |
|-----|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| 0 | Send personal email + LinkedIn DM | Send mail merge | Send mail merge |
| 7 | Follow-up "right person?" | Follow-up | (none) |
| 14 | Break-up + offer asset | Break-up | (none) |
| 21 | Mark cold, archive | Mark cold | Mark cold |

After day 21, do not re-engage for 90+ days unless they reply.

## What to send when a supplier says "show me what you mean"

Pre-build before you start outreach:
1. Single-page HTML wireframe showing the contractor's view with the supplier's product codes prominent on every BOM line
2. One-page overview PDF (problem → solution → distribution model → screenshots)
3. Optional: a 60-second loom showing the calculator in action

Having these ready means you can respond within an hour of any interested reply — which is often the difference between a call and a fade-out.

## When to skip the playbook

- If you have ≤10 named contacts you actually know → just send personal emails, no tiering needed
- If the campaign is informational (research interview, not sales) → use a different opening; this playbook is calibrated for "want to talk to you" pitches
- If your category is regulated (financial services, healthcare) → check compliance for cold email rules before sending
