Anyfence — booking flow

What happens after the customer hits "Book this job" or "Book materials pickup" on the calculator. Five steps: your details → walkthrough video → pick date → review & deposit → booked. Single supplier (Amazing Fencing) so there's no contractor selection step — the moment they hit Book they're booking Amazing Fencing's install team or pickup desk. Stripe-Checkout-style layout: quote summary pinned on the left, active step on the right, persistent progress bar across the top.

Route: /book/:quoteId
Entry from: Entry-page bubble CTA (Stage 5 or 6)
Supplier: Amazing Fencing
Two paths: Supply + install (5 steps) · Supply only (4 steps, skips video)

1 Your details

Quote on the left, contact form on the right.

Customer just clicked "Book this job" on the calculator. Now they're on amazingfencing.com.au/book/q_4f9a2c. Site address is autofilled from the address bar on the entry page. They fill in name, email, phone. Continue → walkthrough video.

Layout

  • Same header as calculator: Amazing Fencing brand + "BOOKING" tag + powered-by-Anyfence strip
  • Progress bar across the top — 5 steps, current step is "Your details"
  • Left ~35%: quote summary (mini map + recap + line totals + Amazing Fencing supplier card)
  • Right ~65%: active step content (form fields)

Quote summary card

  • Mini map thumbnail showing the drawn fence with posts (same canvasEngine rendering as the entry page, scaled down to 200×150)
  • Fence type · dimensions · posts · gates · height — pulled from the canonical_payload
  • Line breakdown: Supply / Install / Extras / Total / 10% deposit
  • Amazing Fencing supplier strip at the bottom — branding consistency

Form fields

  • Site address — autofilled, greyed (the address bar from the entry page)
  • Full name — required
  • Email — required; receives quote PDF + booking confirmation
  • Phone — required; installer texts an hour before arrival
  • Best time to call — optional

Persistence

  • If user backs out mid-flow, draft saves to localStorage + Postgres against the quoteId
  • Email of "quote saved · pick up where you left off" link if user provides email

2 Walkthrough video

"Walk your fence line. Show the installer what they're walking into."

The trust step. Customer records a 60–90 second video showing fence line, neighbouring fences, slope, trees, access. Installer watches it before accepting — cuts site visits for ~70% of jobs and lets Amazing Fencing schedule a 2-day install with confidence. Supply-only skips this entire stage.

Why the video matters

  • Installer sees ground conditions, neighbour-fence condition, tree roots, access for materials
  • Cuts site visit out of the loop for ~70% of straightforward jobs
  • If installer flags issues after watching, deposit refunds + scheduling slot opens back up

Upload UX

  • Big ember-orange drop-zone — primary action
  • Two ways to provide: Record now (camera in-browser) or Upload (file from camera roll)
  • While uploading: progress bar, file size, "uploading…" message
  • Once uploaded: thumbnail + replace / delete options + Continue button enables

Skip option

  • "I'll send this later" link below the upload zone
  • Caveat shown: installer requires the video before accepting — skipping holds the booking until video lands
  • Reminder email goes out 24h later if still missing

Supply-only skip

  • If the customer's mode is Supply only (pickup at depot), this entire step is skipped
  • Progress bar shows step 2 as dashed/struck-through
  • Flow goes directly: details → pickup date → review + deposit → booked
  • See Supply-only diff at the bottom

3 Pick install date

Amazing Fencing's actual calendar. Green days are open, hatched days are booked.

No third-party booking-tool. The calendar shows Amazing Fencing's own install team availability for the customer's postcode (travel time pre-baked). Picking a date locks a tentative slot for 24h — long enough for the installer to watch the video and confirm.

What's on the calendar

  • Available (green) — installer has a 2-day window starting this date, and travel time from the depot is OK
  • Booked (hatched grey) — already taken
  • Closed (red-tinted) — public holidays, weekends if no Saturday team
  • Selected (ember) — the customer's pick; locks tentatively for 24h

Calendar drives the data

  • Pulled from Amazing Fencing's install team's availability_windows (per postcode-zone)
  • Pre-considers travel time from the depot (Currimundi) — far suburbs only show on Tue/Wed/Thu when full-day install crews go out
  • 2-day jobs need 2 consecutive available days — calendar greys out single-day slots

Booking confirmation card

  • Below the calendar: live readout of the selected slot
  • Tue 16 June · 2 day install · finish by Thu 18 June
  • "Tentative — locks for 24h. Installer confirms after watching your video."

5 Booked!

Big green tick. What happens next, in plain English.

The terminal screen. Customer sees the booking is locked, gets a receipt email, and has clear next steps so they don't sit there wondering "now what?". The "Add to calendar" + "View booking" links keep this surface useful long after the customer has tapped through.

Tone

  • Calm + clear. Not over-celebratory (no confetti, no balloons)
  • Tradies booking a $4k job want confidence, not a party
  • Hero icon + headline does the celebrating; everything below is informational

What's on screen

  • Hero: green tick + "Booked!" + sub-text confirming date
  • Next-steps block: 4 numbered cards walking through what happens until install day
  • Reference number: Q-4F9A2C — for support calls / emails
  • Three CTA buttons: View booking · Add to calendar · Email a copy

"What happens next" — 4 cards

  • ① Now: confirmation email + receipt sent
  • ② Within 4 working hours: Amazing Fencing reviews the video + locks the slot or comes back with questions
  • ③ 2 days before: installer calls to confirm access + dropoff spot
  • ④ Day of install: text with team's ETA + job site phone number

What's NOT here

  • No "Browse more fence types" or upsells — they've just booked, don't sell more
  • No share-on-social — tradies don't tweet about their fence
  • No newsletter signup — they're a customer now, that's enough

→ Supply-only diff

If they came in via "Book materials pickup" — four steps, no video.

When the customer's mode on the calculator is Supply only, they're picking up materials from the depot — no install crew, no video needed. The booking flow loses the walkthrough video step and the install date becomes a pickup date.

Step count drops to 4

  • ① Your details — same as install path
  • ② Walkthrough video — SKIPPED (progress dot shown dashed/struck-through)
  • ③ Pick pickup date — pulled from Amazing Fencing's depot availability (no postcode-zone filtering)
  • ④ Review + deposit — 10% deposit; balance on pickup
  • ⑤ Booked — confirmation references pickup, not install

What changes per step

  • Step 3 calendar: shows pickup slots (morning, afternoon, all-day), not 2-day install windows
  • Step 4 review cards: "Your pickup date" replaces "Your install date"; no "Your walkthrough" card
  • Step 4 deposit copy: "Refundable until 24h before pickup" instead of 48h
  • Step 5 next-steps: shorter — just pickup-day text, no installer call

Progress bar visual

  • 5 steps still visible in the progress bar (consistency)
  • Step 2 (walkthrough video) shows as --skipped — dashed border, italic struck-through label
  • Customer knows they skipped it (in case they switch modes)

What's not in v1 of the booking flow
Mobile breakpoint

Quote summary collapses to a top-of-page accordion ("Tap to see your quote"). Calendar uses a date-picker rather than a full month grid. Form fields stack. The deposit banner stays visible above the card form. Separate wireframe pass.

Customer-side post-booking surface

The "View your booking" link from Stage 5 opens /booking/Q-4F9A2C — a persistent page customers come back to. Shows status (Confirmed / Installer-reviewing / Confirmed / Day-of-job / Complete), can re-upload video, message Amazing Fencing, cancel within refund window. Separate wireframe.

Supplier-side review surface

Amazing Fencing's installer team gets a notification with the BOM, video, calendar slot. They approve, request changes, or escalate. Lives at /admin/bookings/queue. Not in this customer-facing wireframe.
